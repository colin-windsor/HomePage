The toilet: if it is noisy

Sometimes the toilet is noisy, and dribbles water into the basin. There is no overflow, if the ball valve is not closing properly the wasted water flows down into the basin.

The top knob pulled for flushing unscrews. It may be necessary to use thin plyers to held the stem. The knob removed, the 1" diameter plastic nut below it can be unscrewed using an adjustable wrench. The float valve slides up and down the valve on the left. The washer seems flexible and reliable, but the cylindrical float and its mechanism gets chaulked up and fails to move freely. The normal water level is very clear from a chaulk rim and if the level is much above this some attention is needed.

Much of the chalk can be removed from the float and mechanism with the fingers. If this fails the mechanism can be unscrewed using the large knurled nut. A wrench is necessary as if it is not tightly screwed up the mechanism will leak! When unscrewed the mechanism can be removed by twisting it about to free it from the float. It can then be cleaned or soaked in killrock. To replace, put the rubben ring in first with the red post fitted into the indetentaion on the mechanism.

We have placed a plastic bottle in the basin to reduce the flush a little.
